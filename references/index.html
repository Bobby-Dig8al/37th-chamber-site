Roots

honor the root · protect the branch

We always cite and reference — to honor the root and protect the branch off main. We don’t care what corporations or lawyers think of our work, unless they’re helping us. We care what the artists who inspired it think, and we’d love, more than anything, for all of them to see it. Every mark here is a © pointed the right way — back at the maker.

A living document — thirty-six branches, one trunk, roots that don’t end. It grows whenever we make an important citation; we cross-link to the source wherever we can, and to the free, whole paper wherever it exists. Take us to the root. Last updated: June 2026.

Physics & Cosmology the frontier

  • The Kerr Metric — Roy Kerr, 1963. Two pages that govern every spinning black hole. The 1963 Phys. Rev. Lett. sits behind a journal door; Kerr’s own free account · arXiv:0706.1109 (opens in new tab)open.
  • Gravitational Lensing by Spinning Black Holes (Gargantua) (opens in new tab)open — James, von Tunzelmann, Franklin & Thorne, 2015. The physics from the Interstellar render (the published CQG version is gated; this is the free one).
  • On the Electrodynamics of Moving Bodies (opens in new tab)open — Einstein, 1905. The founding special-relativity paper.
  • Around-the-World Atomic Clocks (opens in new tab)open — Hafele & Keating, 1972 (Science). The airplane time-dilation experiment — the journal copy is paywalled, this one is free.
  • Relativity in the Global Positioning System (opens in new tab)open — Neil Ashby, 2003 (Living Reviews in Relativity). The GPS in your pocket.
  • First M87 Event Horizon Telescope Results (opens in new tab)open — EHT Collaboration, 2019. The first photograph of a black hole.
  • Observation of Gravitational Waves (GW150914) (opens in new tab)open — LIGO/Virgo, 2016. Two black holes, heard.
  • The Road Toward Imaging a Black Hole (opens in new tab)open — Heino Falcke, 2022. An EHT co-founder on how the M87* image was made.
  • Wormholes in Spacetime and Their Use for Interstellar Travel (opens in new tab)gated — Morris & Thorne, 1988 (AJP). The traversable-wormhole paper Sagan’s Contact prompted into existence — still behind the journal door.
  • The Particle Problem in the General Theory of Relativity (opens in new tab)gated — Einstein & Rosen, 1935. The Einstein–Rosen bridge.
  • The Wormhole · Time Dilation · Gravitational Lensing · Kip Thorne — the deep-dives, each with its own roots.

Dune how a planet became an argument

  • Poverty Grass — Frank Herbert, 1957. An unfinished article about the moving sands of the Oregon coast became the first science-fiction novel built around an ecosystem.
  • The Voice of Arrakis — Hans Zimmer turned down a Nolan film, built instruments that did not exist and a vocal language no one had sung, then checked the sound against real desert.
  • The Stillsuit — the thermodynamics that proves Arrakis is real engineering: a closed water loop held against a 350 K desert.
  • Shai-Hulud — the sandtrout–worm–spice–empire closed loop. Pull one thread and the whole civilization unravels.
  • May Be Dangerous to Your Health — Herbert wrote Paul as a warning, not a hero, and was disturbed when readers cheered. The wheel underneath the story.
  • The Fremen Were Not a Costume — the real Arabic and Islamic scholarship beneath the Fremen: a structural synthesis Herbert studied for years, not a desert costume.
