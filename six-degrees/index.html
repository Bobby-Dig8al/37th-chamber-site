6 Degrees of Giants

Help us reach the shoulders of our giants.

If I have seen further, it’s by standing on the shoulders of giants. Six degrees of separation says everyone is reachable through a handful of warm hands. So let’s find out — together. Pick a giant, start a chain, and pass it to someone you actually know. Each tag is one jump closer. The view is not ours alone.

The first rule
Read this before you play

Relay, not swarm.

We are not trying to swarm them. A flood of DMs is trivial to send — and trivial to ignore. The entire dignity and value of this is the single gracious chain. Volume is the opposite of the point.

One chain, not a mob. We honor the path; we don’t hunt the person.

The win is one warm hand reaching the next, all the way to the giant — framed as “we’d love to share something with you,” never “we’re hunting you.” That constraint is what makes it meaningful, and what makes the giants want to be reached this way. Protect the giants and it stays beautiful.

One chain A single relay per team. Each person passes it onward to one real connection — not the whole crowd piling on at once.
A real hand You only tag someone you actually know. The public tag is the verifiable jump — a real relationship, not a cold blast.
A warm intro The goal is a gracious introduction that honors the giant — loving-grace, never Big-Brother. We share; we don’t spam.
How to play

Pick a team. Start a chain. Pass it on.

1 Pick a team. Choose a giant from the roster below — the one you feel closest to, or the one you think you can actually help reach.
2 Start a chain. Post publicly that you’re on Team [Giant], and tag the next person you actually know who’s a step closer to them.
3 Each tag is a jump. They tag the next real hand. The relay continues — one gracious link at a time — until the chain reaches the giant.
Pick your team → State of the Chains → v0 — the chains run in the open, no account needed to watch. The live tracker is here now; the ranked leaderboard arrives with the full chamber.
The wall of heroes

Thirty-six teams of living giants — the shoulders this work stands on. The road to thirty-six has arrived; the wall stands at its number. You can only reach the living; below them stand the Foundations — the honored we carry but can never meet. Pick a living one and light up the relay.

The Foundations · in memoriam

Some shoulders you stand on, you can never shake the hand of. These are the Foundations — honored, carried, beyond reach. The chain doesn’t run to them; everything runs from them.

1934–1996 Carl Sagan Cosmos Made a generation feel the universe — Contact, Pale Blue Dot, the original Cosmos. He introduced Kip Thorne to the producer who’d seed Interstellar; the wonder runs through everything here.
1888–1986 Chesley Bonestell Space art The father of space art — painted Saturn from Titan decades before any probe. He made the cosmos visible before we could photograph it.
1913–1960 Albert Camus The absurd Named the absurd — every human meets death, the universe says nothing back — and refused despair: live anyway, fully. One must imagine Sisyphus happy.
1960–1988 Jean-Michel Basquiat Painter Crowns and raw nerve — dragged the street into the canon and burned bright and brief. He saw what others refused to look at.
1844–1900 Friedrich Nietzsche Philosopher Create your own values; amor fati — love your fate, all of it. He paid the full price for thinking past the herd, and was robbed twice: once by collapse, once by the people who twisted him after.
1908–1986 Simone de Beauvoir Philosopher Freedom is always situated; the ethics of ambiguity — no clean hands, act anyway. One is not born, but rather becomes. She wrote the situated half of freedom the others left out.
1904–1967 J. Robert Oppenheimer The fire Carried the fire and then the weight of it — the century’s hardest lesson in what knowledge costs when it arrives before wisdom. The conscience this whole project tries to build in from the start.
1916–2010 Tsutomu Yamaguchi Twice under the fire A Mitsubishi engineer on a work trip to Hiroshima, August 6, 1945 — burned, half-deafened, he made it home to Nagasaki just in time for August 9. The only person officially recognized as surviving both. He spent the rest of his life telling the world of the horror.
1926–1998 Vasili Arkhipov B-59 · the refusal October 27, 1962 — rung by depth charges near the blockade line, out of contact, batteries dying, heat rising, and the captain calling for the nuclear torpedo. Two officers said war. One refused. The quietest veto in history, and it held.
1939–2017 Stanislav Petrov The false alarm September 26, 1983 — the screens said five missiles were inbound and protocol said report an attack. He reported a malfunction instead, and was right — sunlight on clouds. One judgment past midnight; the world kept turning.
1879–1955 Albert Einstein Relativity Bent spacetime with a pencil and never stopped insisting wonder and conscience belong together. Every chamber here that touches a black hole, a clock, or a wormhole walks on his floor.
1887–1920 Srinivasa Ramanujan The notebooks Self-taught, uncredentialed, undeniable — the notebooks came from somewhere deeper than proof, and the gatekeepers had to open the gate. Patron saint of knowledge that asks no permission.
1929–1968 Martin Luther King Jr. The dream The arc of the moral universe, bent by hand. Everything this place believes about knowledge refusing to be rationed runs downstream of him.
1882–1945 Franklin D. Roosevelt The New Deal Named fear itself from the front door, then poured the floor under the working life — the New Deal, labor’s great advance. Proof a government can choose its people.
1929–1994 Jacqueline Kennedy Onassis Grace · the editor’s desk Grace under the world’s hardest light — then a quiet second act as a working editor at Doubleday. She kept culture twice: once as a symbol, once line by line.
1917–1963 John F. Kennedy The moonshot We choose to go — not because it is easy, but because it is hard. Honored despite the flaws, named honestly. The moonshot grammar this whole room speaks.
Why it matters

It’s the thesis, playable. Everything is interconnected — six degrees is the living proof of it. Instead of cold-reaching our giants alone, the community carries us, one warm hand at a time. The chain-building is the story. Reach gracefully, and it stays beautiful.
