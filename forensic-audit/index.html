Forensic Audit

collect · trace · report

AI output is confident by default. Confidence is not correctness. I find the gap before it costs you.

Where this practice comes from

I caught the fabrications myself — a citation to a real paper that doesn’t say what the AI claims it says; a figure traced back to nothing; language delivered with complete confidence that dissolves on contact with the source. No malfunction to notice, no warning to read. The fabrication looked exactly like the help. The same machinery writes citations, dosage ranges, contract language, and financial figures with the same straight face. The rule I built from that is the rule I now sell: nothing load-bearing ships unverified.

The thesis

AI multiplies how much you can produce. It also multiplies your unverified claims — silently, at a rate your attention cannot match. The scarce skill is no longer producing the work. It is specifying it precisely and verifying what comes back. That second half is the audit.

What an audit actually is

Step one

Collect

The AI outputs you ship or rely on — reports, briefs, summaries, emails, code, filings. Whatever carries your name out the door.

Step two

Trace

Every load-bearing claim traced back to a source — or flagged as unsourced. Does the cited paper exist? Does it say what the output says it says? Does the number trace to anything at all?

Step three

Report

What’s verified, what’s confabulated, what’s unverifiable — severity ranked by consequence, in plain language you can act on.

No proprietary-tool mystique. The method is the product — and you keep it. An engagement should leave you able to run the discipline yourself on everything that ships after I’m gone.

Who this is for

Professionals shipping AI-assisted work under liability — where the cost of one wrong claim is wildly out of proportion to the seconds it took to generate.

LawOne fabricated citation in a filing doesn’t cost you the brief — it costs you standing with the court.
MedicineA wrong dosage range in patient-facing material isn’t a typo. There is no version of that error that stays small.
Real estateA wrong disclosure survives the closing — and waits. The transaction ends; the liability doesn’t.
FinanceA confident wrong figure compounds twice: once in the decision it drives, once in front of the regulator who finds it.
