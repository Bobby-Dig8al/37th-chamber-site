37th Chamber — How the light is made

The EUV Light Source

Every leading-edge chip is printed with 13.5 nm extreme-ultraviolet light. There is no lamp that makes it — you have to vaporize a falling drop of tin into a star and catch what it screams. Scrub one firing cycle below. In a real machine this repeats ~50,000 times a second.

The droplet falls

A generator pushes molten tin through a nozzle, breaking the stream into identical droplets about 25–30 µm wide — a third the width of a hair — falling at roughly 70 m/s.

Two lasers, not one: a weak pre-pulse flattens the sphere into a thin pancake so the powerful CO₂ main pulse couples into it efficiently. EUV is absorbed by air and glass alike — so from this collector onward, the entire path to the wafer is mirrors in a vacuum.
