the sticky scene ↓

Sticky figureThe figure holds while the captions advance

Below: a figure pins to the viewport while a column of captions scrolls past it. The active caption drives the figure — here, a single mote climbing outward through the shells of an atom. Pure CSS does the pinning; the engine only chooses which caption is live and tells the figure.

01 · n = 1

At rest, the electron sits in the innermost shell — the ground state. Closest to the nucleus, lowest energy. Watch the figure hold here as you begin.

02 · n = 2

Feed it a photon of exactly the right energy and it jumps outward to the second shell. The figure answers: the mote climbs, the live ring lights blue. Energy is quantized — only certain orbits are allowed.

03 · n = 3

A larger kick lifts it to the third shell — excited, unstable. It won't stay. The further out, the looser the nucleus holds it.

04 · emission

It falls back toward the core, and the energy it sheds leaves as a single photon of a precise color. That is a spectral line — the fingerprint we read across light-years. The figure returns home.

The same scene works on a phone: the figure pins to the top of the screen and the captions scroll beneath it. And with motion reduced, the figure simply flows inline above its captions — every step visible, the gold edge still marking the active idea without a single animation.
